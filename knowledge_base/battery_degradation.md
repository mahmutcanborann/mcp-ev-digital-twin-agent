# Battery Degradation Knowledge Base

Lithium-ion battery degradation is commonly reflected by a decrease in State of Health (SOH), reduced usable capacity, lower driving range, and increased internal resistance.

A battery with SOH below 80% is often considered degraded for EV operation and should be monitored more closely.

Low SOH may indicate capacity fade, aging, repeated charge-discharge stress, elevated temperature exposure, or high charging stress.

High temperature can accelerate battery degradation and may increase long-term risk.

Frequent fast charging and high state-of-charge operation may increase degradation risk.

Maintenance recommendations for degraded batteries include closer monitoring, limiting high-stress charging behavior, avoiding excessive heat exposure, and considering replacement when SOH becomes critically low.

Fleet-level anomaly detection can identify batteries that behave differently from the rest of the fleet based on SOH, cycle count, and temperature.
